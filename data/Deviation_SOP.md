# 1.0 OBJECTIVE

The purpose of this document is to describe the requirements and procedures for the Deviation and CAPA Management System. It ensures that nonconformities are documented, evaluated, and mitigated to protect product quality.

## 2.0 SCOPE

*   **Applicability:** Applies to all internal Technical Operations and CMO/CTL entities.

*   **Exclusions:** Does not apply to Preclinical research or Environmental/Utility Monitoring Excursions.

*   **Prohibition:** Planned deviations are strictly prohibited.

## 5.0 PROCEDURE

### 5.1 Initiation and Containment

1. **Clock-Start**: Initiate within **2 business days** of discovery and populate impacted lot numbers.

2. **Language**: All records must be in **English** (Exemption: Austria BioLife centers).

## 5.2 Risk-Based Leveling (The Smart Logic)

Leveling is determined based on **Frequency** and **Severity**.

* **Retrospective Review**: Conduct a lookback to identify repeating deviations.

* **Default Classification**: If a deviation is not listed in the standard reference lists, it is considered a **Level 1** by default, unless re-classified by Quality.

* **L2 Trigger**: Deviations involving critical process parameters, sterility compromises, or falsified data are always Level

* **Default**: If an issue is not listed in Attachment 4, it is a **Level 1** by default.

* **Logic Trap**: "Close Reference Deviations" are always **Level 1**.

* **Override**: Quality Management may re-classify any L1 to L2 based on evaluation.

## Level 2 Investigation and CAPA

1. **Root Cause Analysis**: Utilize investigational tools to identify the primary cause.

2. **Impact Assessment**: Evaluate impact on materials, validated state, regulatory filings, and compliance.

3. **CAPA Plan**: Develop actions to address the root cause and prevent recurrence.

4. **Effectiveness Check**: Develop specific, measurable criteria to confirm the CAPA worked.
